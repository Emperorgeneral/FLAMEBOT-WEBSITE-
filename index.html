How It Works (Technical)

Signal source

Signals are typically produced by third-party providers and posted in Telegram. FlameBot does not “invent” trades — it automates execution of the messages you choose to follow.

Automation pipeline

Telegram message → desktop ingestion → server queue/state → MetaTrader EA poll → order placement + trade-state reporting.

Failure behavior

Automation is best-effort: network latency, Telegram outages, broker execution rules, and MetaTrader configuration can affect fill quality and timing.

Quick Start Guide

01

Open FlameBot and sign into Telegram

Start with your phone number, wait for the Telegram login code, and enter it inside the desktop app. If your Telegram account uses two-step verification, the app will prompt for your 2FA password next.

02

Finish account verification

After Telegram login succeeds, FlameBot verifies your account with the server, assigns your FlameBot profile, and pulls the settings needed for your active platform.

03

Connect your MetaTrader terminal

Install the included EA in MT4 or MT5, allow automated trading, and make sure any required WebRequest URL is whitelisted in the terminal settings before expecting live execution.

04

Select the groups you want to follow

Once the Telegram session is active, FlameBot loads your available groups and channels so you can choose the sources you want the app to listen to for incoming signal messages.

FAQ

Do I need a referral link on the website?

No. The website stays focused on product information, downloads, and setup guidance.

What happens if Telegram asks for extra security?

If Telegram asks for extra verification, that is a normal Telegram security step. FlameBot does not collect or store Telegram passwords, and we have no access to your Telegram credentials.

Why is my EA not placing trades yet?

Check automated trading permissions, terminal WebRequest settings, broker rules, and whether the desktop app has fully finished its first-time verification and configuration sync.
